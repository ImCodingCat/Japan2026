MOST DANGEROUS PLACES IN JAPAN

DON QUIJOTE
THE ULTIMATE IMPULSE BUY TRAP
Don don don ♪ donki ~ ♪ donki ~ houte ~ ♪
• Dangerous for: Anyone who loves random, quirky, and discounted items.
• Why? This mega-store sells everything from cheap souvenirs to high-end cosmetics, luxury watches, and even snacks you didn't know you needed. The chaotic layout makes you buy more than you planned.

AKIHABARA (TOKYO)
OTAKU & TECH HAVEN
• Dangerous for: Your wallet if you're into anime, gaming, or electronics.
• Why? Akihabara is filled with multi-story stores selling rare anime merchandise, high-end gaming gear, and collectible figurines. It's easy to go in for "just one thing" and leave with bags full of expensive goods.

SHINSAIBASHI (OSAKA)
ENDLESS SHOPPING STREETS
• Dangerous for: People who get easily lost in shopping heaven.
• Why? This area is filled with department stores, international brands, and local boutiques. The shopping arcade is so massive that you can literally shop until you drop.

GINZA (TOKYO)
LUXURY OVERLOAD
• Dangerous for: Your bank account if you love designer brands.
• Why? This is Tokyo's premier luxury shopping district, home to flagship stores of Gucci, Chanel, Louis Vuitton, and more. Even window shopping can be dangerous because everything is so tempting.

HARAJUKU (TOKYO)
FASHION FRENZY
• Dangerous for: Anyone obsessed with trendy or unique street fashion.
• Why? Takeshita Street is packed with quirky boutiques, accessory shops, and cutting-edge fashion trends. You'll want to buy everything, and the crowds can be intense.

NIPPONBASHI (OSAKA)
"DEN-DEN TOWN" FOR COLLECTORS
• Dangerous for: Hardcore anime and manga fans.
• Why? Osaka's answer to Akihabara, this place is filled with rare figures, retro games, and expensive collectibles. It's easy to spend a fortune without realizing it.
