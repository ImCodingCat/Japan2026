Japan Travel Guide - Must Know Before You Go

Best Places to Visit
- Tokyo (3-4 Days) – Capital city with shrines, markets, and skyline views.
- Kyoto (2-3 Days) – Temples, geisha streets, bamboo groves.
- Osaka (2 Days) – Street food, nightlife, Osaka Castle.
- Nara (Half Day) – Deer park and Todai-ji Temple.
- Hakone (1-2 Days) – Hot springs, Lake Ashi, Mount Fuji views.
- Hiroshima & Miyajima (1-2 Days) – Peace Memorial Park and floating Torii Gate.
- Hokkaido (3-4 Days) – Nature, skiing, and flower fields.
- Okinawa (2-3 Days) – Beaches, coral reefs, and island culture.

Ideal Itineraries
If You Have 7 Days
- Tokyo (3 Days): Asakusa, Shibuya, Meiji Shrine, teamLab Planets.
- Kyoto (3 Days): Fushimi Inari, Arashiyama, Gion.
- Osaka (1 Day): Dotonbori, Osaka Castle, food tour.

If You Have 10 Days
Everything above plus:
- Hakone (1 Day): Lake Ashi, hot springs, Mount Fuji views.
- Hiroshima & Miyajima (2 Days): Peace Park, Itsukushima Shrine.

If You Have 14 Days
Add:
- Hokkaido (3 Days): Sapporo, Furano, Niseko.
- Nara (Half Day): Deer park & temples.
- Okinawa (2 Days): Beaches & snorkeling.

Seasons & Weather
- March-May: Cherry blossoms and mild weather.
- June-July: Rainy season in most regions.
- July-September: Hot and humid.
- October-November: Cool and colorful — autumn foliage season with red and gold leaves.
- December-February: Cold and snowy — best for skiing in Hokkaido and Nagano.

Sakura Season (Cherry Blossoms)
- Blossoms start in the south (early March) and reach the north (early May).
- Peak bloom: 7-10 days only.
- Forecasts vary yearly — check Japan Meteorological Corp Sakura Forecast close to travel.

Local Transport Tips:
- Trains/subways are punctual and clean; taxis are convenient but pricey.
- Tokyo Metro has multiple operators — study maps before traveling.
- Major Passes:
  - Tokyo Subway 24/48/72-hour Pass – unlimited metro rides.
  - Osaka Amazing Pass – covers transport + key attractions.
  - Kyoto Bus & Subway Pass – ideal for city sightseeing.
  - Keihan Pass – connects Kyoto, Osaka, and Nara.
- Luggage Tip: Trains have limited luggage space — use Yamato's Takkyubin (door-to-door luggage delivery) or travel light.

Navigation:
Use Google Maps or Japan Transit Planner for real-time train info and routes.

Money & Connectivity
- Many smaller restaurants and shrines are cash-only. Use 7-Eleven or Japan Post ATMs.
- Credit cards work widely in cities, less so in rural areas.
- Restaurants rarely split bills and tipping isn't practiced — pay exact amount at the counter.
- Public Wi-Fi is limited — rent a Pocket Wi-Fi or buy a Japan eSIM.
- Buy SIM cards at 7-Eleven after immigration at Narita.
- Transport tip: Check Narita Airport Transport Timetable for train/shuttle details.

Must Try:
Sushi • Ramen • Tempura • Okonomiyaki • Soba • Matcha desserts • Mochi • Sake • Japanese whiskey

Other Essentials
- Public toilets: Clean and found everywhere, even in train stations and convenience stores.
- Tax-free shopping: Show your passport at billing for immediate cash refund (no airport claim).
- Advance bookings: Reserve attractions like teamLab Planets or Kyoto Geisha shows 1-3 months ahead.
- Visit Japan Web: Complete entry details before arrival — vjw.digital.go.jp.
- Kyoto-Osaka: Use the Hankyu Kyoto Train Garaku for easy travel.
- Currency: Carry some Japanese Yen, though cards are increasingly accepted.

Stay Areas:
- Tokyo: Shinjuku or Shibuya for convenience
- Kyoto: Gion for culture and temples
- Osaka: Namba for nightlife and food
- Try Ryokans (traditional inns) or capsule hotels for unique stays.
- Spend at least 4-5 days in Tokyo and 3-4 in Kyoto for a balanced itinerary.

Learn a Few Words
- "Arigato" – Thank you
- "Sumimasen" – Excuse me / sorry
- "Konnichiwa" – Hello
- "Eigo wakarimasen" – I don't understand English
Locals deeply appreciate even a small effort to speak their language.
