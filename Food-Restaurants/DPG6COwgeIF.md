**Audio/Speech Summary:**

The speaker provides a list of important tips for first-time visitors to Japan:

1.  **Escalator etiquette:** In Tokyo and Kyoto, stand on the left (the right side is for walking/passing), but in Osaka/Kansai area, stand on the right (the left side is for passing).
2.  **Convenience store payment:** When paying cash at a konbini, place the money on the tray provided, don't hand it directly to the cashier.
3.  **Toilet use:** Generally, flush tissue paper down the toilet—don't throw it in a trash bin (as marked on the signage).
4.  **Trash disposal:** Trash cans are rare on Japanese streets, so bring small trash bags with you when leaving the hotel.
5.  **Restaurant queue/etiquette:** Line up outside the food stall/restaurant. Adults are generally expected to order a minimum of one menu item each (no sharing meals, though sharing may be allowed for children).
6.  **Public transit:** Always queue (line up) orderly for all public transportation.
7.  **Coins:** You will accumulate a lot of change/coins, some of which (like the 50 Yen coin) have significant value (stated as equivalent to 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah for emphasis). It's recommended to bring a coin purse.
8.  **Fragrances:** Japanese people are sensitive to strong odors, so avoid wearing strong perfumes.
9.  **Noise:** Even in crowded public places, the Japanese are quiet and generally do not shout or make unnecessary noise; tourists should likewise avoid being loud.
