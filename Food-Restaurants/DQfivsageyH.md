DAY 1
Cool Escape to Kibune
10:00AM
Depart from Kyoto Station
Take the subway to Kokusaikaikan Station, then transfer to Kyoto Bus (52) to Kibuneguchi, and again to Bus (33) for Kibune. The trip takes about 1 hour 15 minutes.
11:30AM
Arrive at Kifune Shrine
Hidden in the forested valley of Kibune, this atmospheric shrine is dedicated to the god of water and matchmaking. Follow the lantern-lit path up to the main hall for a serene and spiritual experience.
12:30PM
Lunch at a riverside restaurant
Enjoy lunch at one of Kibune's traditional restaurants built over the river. In summer, you can dine on "kawadoko" platforms suspended above the water-cool, scenic, and uniquely Kyoto.
2:00PM
Kurama-dera Temple
Hike over the forested hill from Kibune to Kurama, passing small shrines and cedar trees. At the top, Kurama-dera awaits with panoramic views and a mystical atmosphere.
3:30PM
Kurama Onsen
Relax in the outdoor rotenburo (open-air bath) surrounded by nature. It's a perfect reward after the hike, offering a taste of rural Kyoto's tranquil side.
7:00PM
Dinner around Demachiyana or Kiyamachi area
Head back to central Kyoto City, choose from cozy izakayas or ramen shops near Demachiyana, or head back toward the Kiyamachi area for more dining options.

DAY 2
Ine Tour with TJT
8:00AM
Hotel Pick-up in Kyoto
We'll pick you up directly from your hotel in Kyoto, then head north through peaceful countryside landscapes toward the scenic coastal regions of northern Kyoto. The drive to Amanohashidate takes around 2.5 hours.
10:30AM
Amanohashidate View Land
Ride a chairlift or monorail up the mountain for sweeping views of Amanohashidate - a pine-covered sandbar stretching across the bay, celebrated as one of Japan's most scenic vistas.
11:30AM
Stroll Along the Sandbar
Walk or rent a bike to explore the 3.6 km long pine-lined sandbar. The refreshing sea breeze and panoramic views make it a peaceful and photogenic highlight.
12:30PM
Lunch at Kaisenkawasaki
A well-loved seafood restaurant in Amanohashidate, known for ultra-fresh local catches. Popular dishes include kaisendon (seafood rice bowls) piled high with seasonal fish, and their signature grilled items.
2:00PM
Drive to Ine Village
Continue your coastal journey with a scenic 30-minute drive to Ine, a charming fishing village known for its unique "funaya" boat houses lining the waterfront.
2:30PM
Ine Bay Boat Tour
Take a short boat tour to see the iconic funaya from the water and learn about local life and traditions-an immersive and relaxing experience.
3:30PM
Explore Ine Town
Wandering along the harbor, this peaceful village offers delightful photo opportunities and lovely cafes, perfect for slowing down and soaking in the atmosphere.
7:00PM
Return to Your Hotel in Kyoto
We will drop you off at your hotel in Kyoto, bringing the day to a comfortable close. You can relax, enjoy a casual dinner nearby, or simply unwind for the night.

DAY 3
From History to Modern Fun
10:00AM
Nijo Castle
Explore this grand former shogunate residence known for its "nightingale floors" and colorful wall paintings. Don't miss the beautifully landscaped Ninomaru Garden.
: 13min walk from Nijojo-mae St.
11:30AM
Kyoto International Manga Museum
Browse thousands of manga from Japan and around the world. You can even sit and read in the courtyard-great for manga fans and curious first-timers alike.
: Karasuma Oike St.
1:00PM
Lunch near Karasuma Oike area
This central neighborhood has a mix of modern cafes and traditional eateries. Try a Kyoto-style lunch set or a Japanese-Western fusion bistro.
3:00PM
Kyoto Aquarium
This family-friendly aquarium focuses on local aquatic life, including the rare giant salamander. Compact but immersive, it's fun and relaxing.
: Umekoji-Kyotonishi St.
6:00PM
Dinner at Kyoto Katsu Gyu
Treat yourself to Kyoto-style wagyu beef katsu, crispy on the outside and juicy inside. A popular spot for meat lovers, offering satisfying meals in a casual setting.
: 5min walk from Kyoto St.
