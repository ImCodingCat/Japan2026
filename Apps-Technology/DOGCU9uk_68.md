## Video Analysis: All Indonesia App for Travelers

This video discusses a new mandatory requirement for travelers entering Indonesia, which involves a unified digital platform.

**1. Text Content:**

* **"Catat ya mulai 01 September 2025 yang"** (Note that starting September 01, 2025, which)
* **"masuk Indonesia wajib isi aplikasi baru ini."** (enter Indonesia must fill out this new application.)

* **"Berlaku di empat pintu masuk utama yaitu Junda, Ngurah Rai dan Batam."** (Applies at four main entry points namely Juanda, Ngurah Rai, and Batam. [Note: Suukarno-Hatta was mentioned in audio but not in the final text overlay for the list.])

**2. Audio/Speech:**

The speaker explains a new mandatory requirement for all travelers entering Indonesia: the use of a new digital application called "All Indonesia."

*   Starting **September 1, 2025**, all travelers entering Indonesia must use this new application.
*   This platform integrates all necessary processes: immigration, customs (Bea Cukai), arrival cards, and even health forms, consolidating them into one digital system.
*   Currently, the system is in effect at four main Indonesian entry points: **Soekarno-Hatta, Juanda, Ngurah Rai, and Batam.**
*   However, by **October 1st**, all international airports in Indonesia will implement this system.
*   The process is simple: travelers can fill out the forms online via the website or the application (All Indonesia) up to three days before arrival.
*   The service is free. Upon completion, travelers will receive a QR code that they can scan at the airport.
*   The speaker advises travelers to fill out the All Indonesia application beforehand to ensure a smooth journey.

**3. Main Message:**

The video's core message is to inform travelers planning to enter Indonesia about the implementation of the new, mandatory, all-in-one digital platform/application called **All Indonesia**, which unifies immigration, customs, and health declaration procedures. Travelers must use this free application/website to complete their arrival card online before landing (up to 3 days prior) to receive a QR code necessary for airport processing, ensuring a smoother entry experience. This mandate is rolling out at major entry points and will be expanded nationwide by October 1st.
